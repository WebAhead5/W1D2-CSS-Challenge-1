Meet Narwhal, the rescuid 'unicorn' puppy with a tail on his face

By Hannah Knowles

November 14, 2019

Narwhal1is by many measures a normal puppy. Caregivers say he likes to nibble on fingers and give kisses and wants to play with anyone nearby. On the other hand, he also has a second tail sprouting from his forehad - an appendage that's promted the nickname "unicorn puppy" and captivated dog lovers in the days since a Missouri rescue group brought him in from the side of a road.

"We are so stupid excited about it," said Rochelle Steffen, who founded the Jackson, Mo.-based organization that's caring for Narwhal, Steffens group, Mac's Mission, focuses on unicorn "special needs" dogs that require extra medical care due to birth defects, injuries or abuse. Staff see horrific cases of pets who've been shot, run over or abandoned, Steffen told The Washington Post. They rescue animals that might get euthanised elsewhere and then help them find permanent homes.
